# Note Writing Protocol

Use the installed `obsidian-markdown` skill for Obsidian syntax and rendering details.

Use this protocol for Zettelkasten substance.

## Source notes

- Record source identity, summary, key sections, key claims, and candidate notes.
- Do not turn authors or source titles into concept notes.

## Literature notes

- Remain source-specific.
- Capture what the source says, not your final theory.
- Include key definitions, arguments, equations, examples, and limitations.

## Concept notes

- Define reusable vocabulary.
- Include definition, core intuition, distinctions, and links with context.
- Good concepts often correspond to named methods, notation, mechanisms, or definitions.

## Permanent notes

- Use claim-shaped titles.
- State one durable idea in your own words.
- Include support, caveats, and links with context.

## Bridge notes

- Explain a meaningful connection between ideas.
- Do not merely list related notes.
- State why the bridge matters.

## Contradiction/tension notes

- Preserve tensions, trade-offs, disagreements, or incompatible claims.
- Not every source requires one.

## Index notes

- Act as maps, not essays.
- Group concepts, permanent notes, bridge notes, contradictions, questions, and source trail.

## Decision logs

Record:

- candidate inventory;
- notes created/updated;
- notes deliberately not created;
- uncertainties;
- next actions.
